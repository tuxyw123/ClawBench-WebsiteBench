Methodology

Six task-design judgments, no composite quality score

The human rubric operationalizes the corpus requirements in WebsiteBench.md. It evaluates whether a task is fit to benchmark web-to-code systems; it does not evaluate a particular candidate implementation.

01

Protocol & observability

Are requirements public or normally observable? Are the PRD, candidate contract, browser policy, scoring contract, and hidden-test support coherent and fair?

WebsiteBench §§3, 8, 12
02

Behavioral depth

Do journeys exercise meaningful state changes, roles, failures, persistence, concurrency, and backend rules instead of a static shell?

WebsiteBench §§4, 6, 9
03

Visual & interaction coverage

Do checkpoints cover representative routes, states, desktop/mobile breakpoints, validation, loading, error, empty, and success interactions?

WebsiteBench §§6, 9.1–9.2
04

Reproducibility

Can seed, reset, controlled time, fixtures, accounts, and evaluation runs be reproduced without dependence on wall-clock waits or drifting services?

WebsiteBench §§4, 6.4, 11
05

Isolation & asset compliance

Does evaluation prevent reference proxying and external dependencies? Are code, content, assets, trademarks, and data sources appropriately licensed?

WebsiteBench §§2, 3.3, 9.5, 13
06

Diversity & non-reskin

Does the task add product-logic or interaction diversity rather than only changing logos, colors, copy, or fixture values within a family?

WebsiteBench §§6.1–6.2, 7

Evidence labels used by this viewer

Automatic readiness

Presence, absence, validity, or non-applicability of declared artifacts. Never weighted into a quality score.

Human task QA

Six explicit ratings plus a gate decision, reviewer, rationale, evidence references, revision, and artifact fingerprint.

Official candidate result

Only a schema-valid websitebench.result.v1 report: one official /100 score and five official dimensions.

Diagnostic visual metrics

Viewer-side screenshot diagnostics for investigation. They do not stand in for the official visual score.

Legacy verifier

Self-verification check counts from compatibility clones. They are not candidate benchmark scores.
